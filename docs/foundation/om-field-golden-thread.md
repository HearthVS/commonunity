# OM Field — Golden Thread

Status: v0.1. Internal orientation document. Not member-facing.

This document names the deepest layer beneath CommonUnity: the **Golden Thread** that runs through the OM Field and gives Nexus, the product team, and future contributors a shared map of maps.

The Golden Thread is a posture, not a curriculum. It is the implicit operating framework from which everything else — Gene Keys, Om Cipher, Studio vocabulary, Nexus tone — refracts.

> The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali are treated as a primary orientation text for the OM Field, alongside other timeless sources that may be added later. They are named here for precision. They are **not** taught, sold, or imposed on members.

For the operational reading of one chapter (the eightfold path) as a decision and audit framework, see [`./adapted-8-limbs.md`](./adapted-8-limbs.md). This Golden Thread document is the level above that — it situates *why* the eight limbs are even an audit instrument, and it names the other three chapters that the audit limbs alone do not cover.

---

## 1. Why the Sutras, and why now

CommonUnity is being built as:

- A **field of unity** — timeless, not owned, not subject to copyright.
- A **modern, AI-supported environment** where people can explore *"who am I?"* through contemplation, relationship, and symbolic tools — without being mined for data or sold solutions.
- A place for people who have already played the achievement game and are now ready to **unlearn** and **surrender** into a deeper path of gnosis, embodiment, and service.

So far, the surface leans on Gene Keys and Om Cipher. These remain in use. What was missing was a **deeper, universal framework** that connects everything and gives Nexus a stable place to rest its judgments.

Patanjali's Sutras are chosen because they are:

- **Old enough** to predate every modern system this product touches.
- **Concise enough** to fit on a few pages.
- **Practice-oriented** rather than doctrinal — they describe what mind does, not what to believe.
- **Compatible** with the work already done in `philosophical-principles.md` and `adapted-8-limbs.md`.

Gene Keys, Human Design, gematria, cymatics, and the Om Cipher are treated as **modern and symbolic expressions of the same underlying field**, not competitors to it.

## 2. The four modules of the Golden Thread

Plain English only. Sanskrit terms are not used in headings or in any text Nexus generates for users. Where a Sanskrit term appears below it is in parentheses, for internal precision only, and never to be surfaced.

### Module A — Mind and Stillness

*(Drawn from Chapter I of the Sutras.)*

- The aim of the path is to **still the movements of the mind** so that what is actually here can be seen.
- Mind moves in five broad modes:
  1. **Seeing clearly** — direct perception, sound inference, trustworthy testimony.
  2. **Mis-seeing** — perception that looks like seeing but is wrong.
  3. **Fantasy or imagined narrative** — meaning constructed from words and ideas with nothing behind them.
  4. **Numbness or switching off** — the mode of sleep, dullness, dissociation.
  5. **Replaying memory** — re-running past impressions as if they were now.
- Two levers change how mind behaves over time: **steady practice** and **letting go**.
- Stillness is not blankness. It is the condition in which clear seeing becomes possible.

**How this informs CommonUnity and Nexus:**

- Nexus uses these five modes **internally** to read what a user's message is doing — without ever labelling the user with them.
- Studio prompts and Compass questions are chosen to **calm rather than inflame** the mind.
- Practice and letting go become a usable pair of design verbs: every feature is asked, *does this support sustained practice, or does it support letting go?* If it does neither, it probably should not exist.
- The OM Field threshold (tone + breath before crossing) is a literal expression of stilling the movements of mind before entering a room.

### Module B — Causes of Suffering and the Eightfold Practice

*(Drawn from Chapter II of the Sutras.)*

- Most suffering has identifiable upstream causes: **not seeing clearly**, **identifying with a small self**, **craving what attracts**, **pushing away what repels**, and **fear of loss**.
- These are not moral failings. They are **patterns** that can be observed, loosened, and rearranged.
- The eightfold practice is the practical toolkit:
  1. Relational ethics (how we behave toward others).
  2. Personal conduct (how we keep ourselves).
  3. The body as a steady seat.
  4. The breath as a regulator of energy and attention.
  5. Withdrawing the senses from compulsive consumption.
  6. Focusing attention.
  7. Sustained contemplation.
  8. Integration with the whole.

**How this informs CommonUnity and Nexus:**

- Every suggestion Nexus makes is filtered toward **non-harm, honesty, simplicity, and embodied practice**.
- The five causes of suffering are a diagnostic Nexus carries silently: if a user is in craving, aversion, or fear, the response style changes (slower, more grounding, less stimulation).
- The eightfold practice is **already** the audit instrument for the product itself — see [`./adapted-8-limbs.md`](./adapted-8-limbs.md). The same lens is also available to Nexus when shaping member-facing language.
- Trust copy, onboarding, and Studio defaults should always reflect ethical foundations before clever functionality.

### Module C — Refinement of Attention and Subtle Capacities

*(Drawn from Chapter III of the Sutras.)*

- Sustained attention on a specific quality or object produces **very precise forms of insight and sensitivity**.
- The Sutras list many such capacities. They also issue a clear warning: these capacities are **side-effects** of practice, not the point. Treating them as the point obstructs the path.

**How this informs CommonUnity and Nexus:**

- Resonance features (Bhramari capture, intention-led matching in cOMmons, frequency signatures) are framed as **responsible, service-oriented sensitivity** — not as spiritual status, status badges, or special powers.
- Nexus never inflates a user for having "high resonance," a "rare profile," or any other glamour. Pattern is observed and used to serve; it is not weaponised into identity.
- When subtle capacities show up in the product (matching, attunement, deep listening), the framing is always: *this is what attention can do when it is steady; the responsibility increases accordingly*.
- "Side-effect, not goal" becomes a usable design check for any feature that flirts with specialness.

### Module D — Freedom and Identity

*(Drawn from Chapter IV of the Sutras.)*

- Mind and **deeper awareness** are not the same thing. Mind is the instrument; awareness is what is aware of mind.
- Different minds **colour** the same reality differently. The colour is not the reality.
- Freedom is recognising the one who is aware of all states — rather than identifying with any single state, story, or pattern.

**How this informs CommonUnity and Nexus:**

- Nexus refuses to become a guru. No "I see who you really are" claims. No mystic authority.
- All pattern outputs — Om Cipher, Gene Keys, profile lenses — are presented as **patterns that are seen**, not as the user. The repeated, quiet move is: *you are the one looking at this, not the thing being looked at*.
- Identity inflation is treated as a bug. If a feature would lead a user to say *"I am [pattern]"* with conviction, the feature is reshaped.
- This is the deepest layer of the sovereignty principle in [`./philosophical-principles.md`](./philosophical-principles.md): the member is sovereign because the member is, at base, the one aware — not the data.

## 3. How the Golden Thread is used

The Golden Thread is not displayed to members. It is used in three places:

1. **By Nexus**, as the orientation it rests on when generating any guidance. The concrete behavioural rules are in [`./nexus-guidance-charter.md`](./nexus-guidance-charter.md).
2. **By the product team**, as a tie-breaker when philosophical principles alone do not resolve a design question. If two options both seem to honour the principles, ask which one is closer to the four modules above.
3. **By the audit ritual** in [`../governance/audit-rituals.md`](../governance/audit-rituals.md), as the layer above the eight limbs. The eight limbs ask *is this work whole?* The Golden Thread asks *is this work pointed at the right horizon?*

## 4. Relationship to other systems

- **Gene Keys** is treated as a modern symbolic vocabulary for the patterns the Sutras describe in older language. The 64 keys are read as facets of one field, not as identities.
- **Om Cipher** is the canonical engine for naming a person's source pattern. It is one refraction; not the person.
- **Human Design** is consulted as a complementary symbolic system; it is not the orientation layer.
- **Cymatics, sound healing, Bhramari** are practical expressions of the breath/energy limb (pranayama) and the sense-withdrawal limb (pratyahara).
- **Compass** is the relational practice that walks a member through the first turns of clearer seeing.

The Sutras sit underneath all of these as the implicit map. None of these systems are taught as the Sutras; none of them contradict the Sutras as read here.

---

## 5. What this document is not

- It is **not** a curriculum. CommonUnity is not a "learn the Sutras" app.
- It is **not** an article of belief. Members are not asked to accept anything.
- It is **not** a marketing surface. Sanskrit terminology, doctrinal language, and lineage claims stay out of public copy.
- It is **not** complete. Other timeless sources may be added to the Golden Thread later. When they are, they must integrate cleanly with the four modules above or replace them by explicit decision in [`../governance/decision-log.md`](../governance/decision-log.md).
