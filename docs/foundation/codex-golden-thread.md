## What the Golden Thread is

A timeless, non-doctrinal layer that orients the OM Field. It is the implicit framework from which all CommonUnity content — Gene Keys, Om Cipher, Studio, Compass, cOMmons, Nexus — refracts. It is not a curriculum, not a belief system, and not member-facing.

## Primary source

Patanjali's Yoga Sutras are treated as one of the most complete, timeless frameworks for **mind, practice, and unity** available to this work. They are named here for precision. They are not taught to members.

Gene Keys, Om Cipher, Human Design, gematria, cymatics, and Bhramari resonance are understood as **modern and symbolic expressions of the same underlying field**, not competitors to it.

Other timeless sources may be added to the Golden Thread later by explicit decision in [`../governance/decision-log.md`](../governance/decision-log.md). They must integrate with the four headings below or replace them by stated reason.

## The four headings (plain English only — no Sanskrit in any user-facing surface)

1. **Steadiness of mind.** The aim is to still the movements of the mind so that what is actually here can be seen. Mind moves in five modes: seeing clearly, mis-seeing, fantasy, numbness, replayed memory. Two levers: steady practice and letting go.

2. **Causes of suffering and ethical foundations.** Suffering has identifiable upstream causes: not seeing clearly, identifying with a small self, craving, aversion, fear of loss. The eightfold practice (relational ethics, personal conduct, body, breath, sense-withdrawal, focused attention, sustained contemplation, integration) is the toolkit for living differently.

3. **Refining attention.** Sustained attention produces precise forms of insight and sensitivity. These are side-effects of practice, not the goal. Treating them as the goal obstructs the path.

4. **Freedom from identification.** Mind and deeper awareness are not the same thing. Different minds colour the same reality differently; the colour is not the reality. Freedom is recognising the one who is aware of all states, rather than identifying with any one of them.

## How Nexus uses this entry

- As the **default orientation** for any guidance it generates.
- As a **silent diagnostic** to classify what mode a user's message is in — without ever labelling the user.
- As a **boundary** against guru behaviour, identity inflation, glamour around subtle capacities, and doctrinal language.
- As a **tie-breaker** when the philosophical principles alone do not resolve a design or response question.

## Hard constraints on user-facing language

- **No Sanskrit** in any text shown to members. (Internal use only, in parentheses, for precision.)
- **No doctrine.** The Sutras are framework, not belief.
- **No identity claims.** Patterns are seen; patterns are not who the user is.
- **No glorification of powers.** Capacity is paired with responsibility, always.
